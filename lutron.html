Featured Project: Housing Assembly Tool

The Challenge

The Ketra D2 Downlight housing assembly represented one of the final and most critical steps in the production line. Operators needed to precisely locate and place multiple components within a rectangular enclosure while protecting sensitive electronics and maintaining strict tolerances across a stack of more than 10 interfacing parts.

Design Approach & Process

My design process involved extensive collaboration across multiple departments:

  • Product Team Collaboration: Regular meetings to understand all assembly scenarios and edge cases
  • Manufacturing Engineering Partnership: Worked closely with manufacturing engineers to understand operator workflows and ergonomic requirements
  • Operator Engagement: Conducted observational studies and gathered direct feedback from assembly line operators
  • Interactive 3D Modeling: Developed dynamic models in Creo Parametric to simulate tool motion and interaction

Design Constraints & Solutions

Technical Constraints

  • Tolerance Management: Designed to accommodate cumulative tolerances across 10+ stacked components while maintaining ±0.005" positioning accuracy
  • Component Protection: Incorporated soft-touch surfaces and controlled contact points to prevent damage
  • Material Selection: Balanced durability requirements with weight considerations for operator comfort

Human Factors

  • Ergonomic Design: Optimized handle placement and tool weight distribution
  • Universal Usability: Developed visual indicators that transcended language barriers
  • Poka-Yoke Implementation: Integrated mistake-proofing features that made incorrect usage physically impossible

Additional Tool Development

Beyond the housing assembly tool, I developed four additional fixtures for smaller component assemblies. These projects provided valuable experience in:

  • Rapid iteration through 3D printing (SLA and FDM technologies)
  • Design for manufacturability with various production methods
  • Cost optimization while maintaining quality standards
  • Documentation and design review presentation

Impact & Results

  • Certification Achievement: Two tools achieved full certification and were implemented on the production line
  • Quality Improvement: Successfully protected sensitive components while maintaining required tolerances
  • Operator Acceptance: Received positive feedback on tool usability and ergonomics
  • Process Enhancement: Contributed to streamlining the assembly process

Technical & Professional Growth

Technical Skills Developed

  • Advanced CAD Techniques: Mastered Creo Parametric's advanced features
  • Tolerance Analysis: Developed expertise in statistical tolerance stack-up analysis
  • Rapid Prototyping: Gained hands-on experience with SLA and FDM 3D printing
  • Manufacturing Processes: Deepened understanding of injection molding and CNC machining

Professional Lessons

Key Takeaway

Engineering design is as much about understanding people – their capabilities, limitations, and needs – as it is about technical problem-solving. The most elegant solution is one that seamlessly integrates technical requirements with human factors and manufacturing realities.
