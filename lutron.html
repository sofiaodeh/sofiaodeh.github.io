Lutron Electronics Co-Op

Mechanical Design Engineering

Location Coopersburg, PA
Duration 6 Months
Year 2024

Overview

During my co-op at Lutron Electronics, I worked with the Tool and Testing Team to develop comprehensive testing fixtures and tools for quality assurance of lighting control systems. My work directly impacted production efficiency and product reliability for the Ketra D2 Downlight assembly line.

I designed and developed assembly tools for the Ketra D2 Downlight, mastering Creo Parametric and rapid prototyping techniques throughout the process.

Exploded view of testing fixture designed in Autodesk Inventor

Engineering Contributions

  • Designed 5+ custom testing fixtures with precise dimensional requirements for automated assembly
  • Applied mechanics of materials principles to ensure fixture durability under cyclic loading
  • Mastered tolerance stack analysis through iterative design and peer review processes
  • Advanced CAD proficiency through complex assembly design in Autodesk Inventor

Skills Developed

  • Advanced 3D printing and machining techniques for rapid prototyping
  • Gained hands-on experience with production-level quality requirements
  • Strengthened understanding of organizational dynamics and cross-team collaboration

Collaboration & Professional Development

I partnered with manufacturing engineers to translate designs into producible solutions. This required communicating my ideas clearly and understanding when to seek input from others and learn from their experience. I always made an effort to make everyone feel heard and ensure their opinion was impactful to my work.

I developed comprehensive documentation systems for all my tools, ensuring someone would be able to continue my work once my co-op ended. This included detailed assembly procedures, maintenance schedules, and design rationale documents.

Learning Experience

My intentions going into the opportunity were to develop as an engineer and apply the theories I had learned in class to real engineering work. On the tool and testing team, I really had to think back to mechanics of materials and develop a way of thinking based on the intuition from those classes.

Learning to do tolerance stacks was an initial challenge since it was a new skill I was developing. As I did multiple iterations, my understanding deepened, and I now feel confident in my ability to do them. Reading guides provided by Lutron and having peers review my work was extremely insightful.

Tools & Technologies

Autodesk Inventor Creo Parametric 3D Printing Rapid Prototyping Tolerance Stack Analysis CAD Design Machining

Impact

My supervisor helped me grow in multiple ways, ranging from technical to interpersonal. I always felt able to ask questions and was guided in the right direction. The balance between receiving directions and having the freedom to work at my pace made this experience truly enjoyable.
