HAB Camera Development

Overview

During my co-op at LineSpect, I worked on developing advanced optical imaging systems, with a primary focus on the HAB (High Altitude Balloon) Camera project. This role combined mechanical engineering, optical design, and hands-on prototyping to create innovative microscopy solutions for environmental monitoring and research applications.

Most Notable Achievements

Complete Redesign of Optical Backplate

  • Stray Light Reduction: Implemented shielding solutions to dramatically improve imaging quality
  • Mirror Mount Simplification: Redesigned mounts for faster and more repeatable optical alignment
  • System Size Optimization: Minimized tube diameter while maintaining full field of view (FOV)
  • Component Integration: Combined NVIDIA mount and backplane into a single 3D-printed component
  • Assembly Refinement: Reduced screw count and part complexity for streamlined manufacturing

Electronics & Manufacturing Excellence

  • Reflow soldered LED boards from scratch and repaired faulty components
  • Conducted rigorous testing of LED drivers to identify issues and prevent overcurrent damage
  • Recovered two critical NVIDIA Orin Nano boards after system shutdown
  • Learned precision glass and mirror cutting techniques for prototype assembly
  • Independently mastered Zemax software for toroidal lens modeling

HAB Camera Development

Optical System Optimization

  • Housing Design: Optimized to fit within 4" copper pipe while maintaining optical performance
  • Optical Path Modeling: Reduced tube size while preserving full field of view

  • Beam Splitter Enhancement: Enabled interchangeability with dichroic mirrors for improved adjustability

Technical Challenges Overcome

The primary challenge involved unreliable electrical components that delayed imaging capabilities and postponed light sheet thickness measurements. Despite these setbacks, I:

  • Developed diagnostic procedures to quickly identify component failures
  • Created workaround solutions to continue testing while awaiting replacements
  • Established preventive maintenance protocols for critical components
  • Documented failure modes to prevent future occurrences

Advice for Future Interns

Key Recommendations:

  • Study Design History: Understand past design changes to better comprehend current configurations
  • Hands-on Assembly: Physically disassemble and reassemble the backplane early to understand the system
  • CAD Best Practices: Maintain consistent reference points for dimensions to allow rapid design changes
  • Printer Maintenance: Routinely perform calibration prints and manually calibrate the FDM base plate every 4 large prints
  • Documentation: Keep detailed records of all modifications and test results
