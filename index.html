About Me

I'm a senior at Northeastern University pursuing a dual degree in Mechanical Engineering and Astrophysics. Currently, I work at the Aramaki Lab analyzing cosmic muon detection for the GRAMS (Gamma-Ray and AntiMatter Survey) experiment while exploring the intersection of art and STEM through my podcast, "The Art of Inquiry."

My engineering journey spans from designing precision optical systems at LineSpect to developing automated testing fixtures at Lutron Electronics. I believe great technical minds are fundamentally artists—a philosophy inspired by figures like Einstein that drives my approach to problem-solving and innovation.

Beyond the lab and classroom, I'm passionate about music (blues, rock, techno, and opera), philosophy, and fashion. These diverse interests inform my technical work, bringing creative perspectives to engineering challenges.
