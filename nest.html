Background: From COBRA to NEST

My journey with this project began with the COBRA (Crater Observing Bio-inspired Rolling Articulator) at the Silicon Synapse Lab, where I focused on integrating Raspberry Pi sensors and enhancing obstacle detection capabilities. The COBRA robot's unique bio-inspired design allows it to navigate challenging lunar terrain through tumbling and sidewinding movements.

Building on this experience, I initiated NEST as a student-led project to advance the COBRA concept. While COBRA demonstrated the viability of bio-inspired locomotion for lunar exploration, NEST takes this further by implementing swarm intelligence and networked communication.

Objectives

Long-term Vision

Develop 5-6 mini-COBRA modules with advanced communication capabilities for enhanced lunar exploration through coordinated swarm behavior.

Current Goal

Build an additional mini-COBRA module to augment the existing one, enabling the software team to develop and test networking protocols.

My Role as Mechanical Lead

As Mechanical Lead for NEST, I oversee the physical realization of our robotic swarm while coordinating closely with electrical and software teams to ensure seamless integration.

Key Responsibilities:

  • 3D Printing Management: Overseeing the fabrication of all essential structural components using FDM technology
  • CAD Design Refinement: Collaborating with team leads to optimize mechanical designs for manufacturability and performance
  • System Integration: Focusing on improved battery tethering solutions and ensuring mechanical compatibility with electrical systems
  • Design Innovation: Adapting the bracket design to allow for untethered battery installation, improving robot autonomy

Current State of Design

The team has successfully built one functional mini-COBRA robot, which serves as our prototype for testing locomotion algorithms and sensor integration. The mechanical design features:

  • Modular segment construction allowing for easy assembly and maintenance
  • Lightweight 3D-printed components optimized for strength-to-weight ratio
  • Flexible joint mechanisms enabling tumbling and sidewinding locomotion
  • Integrated mounting points for sensors and communication hardware

Design Improvements in Progress

Before scaling up production, we're refining the mechanical design to address key challenges:

  • Developing untethered battery mounting system for improved autonomy
  • Optimizing bracket geometry for easier assembly
  • Enhancing structural rigidity while maintaining flexibility
  • Improving wire routing to protect electrical connections during movement

Technical Innovation

NEST pushes the boundaries of lunar exploration technology through several key innovations:

Swarm Intelligence

Unlike traditional single-robot missions, NEST employs multiple coordinated units that can:

  • Share sensor data to create comprehensive environmental maps
  • Distribute tasks based on individual robot capabilities and position
  • Provide redundancy - if one unit fails, others continue the mission
  • Cover larger areas more efficiently than single robots

Bio-Inspired Locomotion

The mini-COBRA design mimics biological movement patterns, enabling navigation through:

  • Tumbling motion for traversing rough terrain
  • Sidewinding for sandy or loose regolith
  • Articulated body segments for navigating tight spaces

Future Development

As we continue developing NEST, our roadmap includes:

  1. Phase 1 (Current): Complete second mini-COBRA unit with improved mechanical design
  2. Phase 2: Implement and test networking protocols between two units
  3. Phase 3: Scale to 5-6 units with full swarm capabilities
  4. Phase 4: Conduct comprehensive field testing in lunar analog environments
